# Authorship and AI-assistance disclosure

## Project ownership

**How Risky Is Today?** was conceived and directed by **Raunaq Sharma** as a policy-research prototype about the visibility of routine urban hazards, evidence transparency and public accountability in Delhi/NCR.

Raunaq Sharma is responsible for the core policy idea, research questions, intended civic purpose, major product decisions, critique and correction of the accountability model, and the substantive report body submitted with the fellowship application.

## AI-assisted work

AI tools, principally OpenAI Codex, were used under the creator's direction to help:

- translate the policy concept into a working web prototype;
- implement and revise application code;
- structure tests, database migrations and scheduled ingestion functions;
- perform interface and quality-assurance iterations;
- prepare development-evidence material and captions;
- generate the explicitly labelled proposed-interface images in Appendix B; and
- assemble this local repository snapshot.

The use of AI assistance does not mean that the AI system originated the project's policy vision. It also does not mean that every generated implementation choice should be treated as the creator's unaided technical authorship.

## Report allocation

- **Main fellowship report body:** written by Raunaq Sharma.
- **Appendix A:** selected, arranged and captioned with AI assistance from existing project files and QA records.
- **Appendix B:** prospective interface concepts created with AI assistance in response to Raunaq Sharma's policy direction.

## Accountability correction directed by the creator

The creator explicitly rejected a design in which an administrator reviews claims and acts as a private source of truth. The revised concept instead relies on identified journalism, official statements and public jurisdiction or ownership records. It distinguishes the authority publicly connected to an incident, the source basis for that connection, publicly reported action, disputes, corrections and the absence of a located public update.

This disclosure is included so reviewers can evaluate both the originality of the policy work and the role of AI-assisted prototyping without ambiguity.
